\begin{abstract}
This document describes the Maude rewriting-logic model of the
\emph{Skyhook} protocol, a Raceboat transport plugin that relays messages
through AWS~S3 cloud storage.  The model captures the full lifecycle
of S3 interactions---bucket creation, public-access-block configuration,
bucket-policy management, and the rolling UUID object-permission
scheme---as they appear in the reference C++ implementation.  Two
module variants are provided: a \emph{nondeterministic} variant for
exhaustive state-space exploration and a \emph{probabilistic/timed}
variant for quantitative simulation within the Maude-HCS experiment
pipeline.  The document is intended for Maude practitioners who wish to
compose the Skyhook model with other protocol models or extend it for
new experimental scenarios.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

Skyhook is a transport plugin for the RACE / Raceboat system.  It enables
two parties---an \emph{Account Holder} who owns AWS credentials and a
\emph{Public User} who does not---to exchange messages via objects stored
in S3 buckets.  Access control is maintained by continuously updating
S3 bucket policies so that only the ``current'' object UUID is readable
or writable by the public, while consumed objects are deleted and their
permissions revoked.

The Maude model presented here abstracts the low-level HTTP/\texttt{curl}
operations into typed S3~API messages and captures:

\begin{itemize}
  \item The \textbf{initialization} sequence (bucket creation,
        \texttt{PutPublicAccessBlock}, baseline policy installation).
  \item The \textbf{rolling UUID queue} that the Account Holder
        maintains for both ``puttable'' and ``fetchable'' object
        permissions.
  \item The \textbf{PublicUser\'{}s stateless S3 interactions}
        (anonymous \texttt{GET}/\texttt{PUT} via pre-signed object
        UUIDs), where the next UUID is derived by hashing the current
        one.
  \item A \textbf{mock AWS~S3 actor} that stands in for the cloud
        service, allowing closed-world simulation.
\end{itemize}

\section{Data Structures}\label{sec:data}

\subsection{StringList}

The C++ implementation maintains \texttt{std::deque<std::string>}
structures for tracking rolling object UUIDs.  In Maude these are
modeled as an associative list with identity:

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={StringList sort},label={lst:stringlist}]
sort StringList .
subsort String < StringList .
op emptyStringList : -> StringList [ctor] .
op _::_ : StringList StringList -> StringList
           [ctor assoc id: emptyStringList] .
\end{lstlisting}

Helper functions mirror the C++ deque interface:

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabular}{@{}llp{7cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Operator} & \textbf{Signature} & \textbf{C++ Equivalent} \\
\midrule
\texttt{head}    & $\texttt{StringList} \to \texttt{String}$      & \texttt{deque::front()} \\
\texttt{tail}    & $\texttt{StringList} \to \texttt{StringList}$   & pop front, return rest \\
\texttt{append}  & $\texttt{StringList} \times \texttt{String} \to \texttt{StringList}$ & \texttt{deque::push\_back()} \\
\texttt{last}    & $\texttt{StringList} \to \texttt{String}$       & \texttt{deque::back()} \\
\texttt{listLen} & $\texttt{StringList} \to \texttt{Nat}$          & \texttt{deque::size()} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{StringList helper functions.}
\end{table}

\subsection{UUID Hashing Abstraction}

The C++ code derives the next object UUID by computing
\texttt{SHA-256(currentUuid)}.  The model abstracts this as:

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={sha256 abstraction}]
op sha256 : String -> String .
eq sha256(STR:String) = STR:String + "-hash" .
\end{lstlisting}

This is a sound abstraction for protocol-level reasoning: it preserves
the key property that successive UUIDs form a deterministic chain from
a given seed, while remaining human-readable in traces.

\section{Rewrite Rules}\label{sec:rules}

This section documents every rewrite rule, grouped by actor.  Each rule
is labeled with its Maude rule name.

\subsection{PublicUser Rules}

The PublicUser is the ``unauthenticated'' side.  It uses anonymous HTTP
to read/write S3 objects at UUIDs that the AccountHolder has made
publicly accessible.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lp{8.5cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Rule Label} & \textbf{Behavior} \\
\midrule
\texttt{pu-handle-fetch-cmd}
  & On receiving \texttt{SkyhookFetchCmd}, emits \texttt{S3GetObjReq} for the current \texttt{fetchObjUuid}. \\
\texttt{pu-handle-incoming-get-res}
  & On \texttt{S3GetObjRes}, advances \texttt{fetchObjUuid} to \texttt{sha256(fetchObjUuid)} and forwards the data to Raceboat via \texttt{SkyhookPostCmd}. \\
\texttt{pu-handle-post-cmd}
  & On receiving \texttt{SkyhookPostCmd(data)}, emits \texttt{S3PutObjReq} at the current \texttt{postObjUuid}. \\
\texttt{pu-handle-incoming-put-res}
  & On \texttt{S3PutObjRes}, advances \texttt{postObjUuid} to \texttt{sha256(postObjUuid)}. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{PublicUser rewrite rules.}
\end{table}

\paragraph{UUID chain property.} After $n$ successful fetches starting
from seed $u_0$, the PublicUser's fetch UUID is
$\text{sha256}^{(n)}(u_0)$.  The AccountHolder must have pre-authorized
this exact UUID, so the two sides stay synchronized through the
deterministic hash chain.

\subsection{AccountHolder Rules}

\subsubsection{Initialization}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lp{8.5cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Rule} & \textbf{Behavior (mirrors \texttt{LinkAccountHolder} constructor)} \\
\midrule
\texttt{ah-handle-start-cmd}
  & Fires when \texttt{puttableUuids} is empty and \texttt{SkyhookStartCmd} arrives from Raceboat.  Emits six messages to \texttt{AwsS3}:
    (1)~\texttt{S3CreateBucketReq} for each bucket,
    (2)~\texttt{S3PutPublicAccessBlockReq} for each bucket,
    (3)~\texttt{S3PutBucketPolicyReq} installing baseline puttable/gettable policies.
    Seeds \texttt{puttableUuids} with \texttt{sha256(initialFetchObjUuid)}. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsubsection{Fetch Path (Receiving Data)}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lp{8.5cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Rule} & \textbf{Behavior (mirrors \texttt{fetchOnActionThread})} \\
\midrule
\texttt{ah-handle-fetch-cmd}
  & Emits \texttt{S3GetObjReq} for \texttt{head(puttableUuids)}, the oldest UUID the public is permitted to write to. \\
\texttt{ah-handle-incoming-get-res}
  & On successful get: (1)~pops the oldest UUID from \texttt{puttableUuids}, (2)~appends a newly hashed UUID, (3)~sends \texttt{S3DeleteObjReq} for the consumed object, (4)~sends two \texttt{S3PutBucketPolicyReq} messages (revoke old, authorize new), (5)~delivers data to Raceboat. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsubsection{Post Path (Sending Data)}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lp{8.5cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Rule} & \textbf{Behavior (mirrors \texttt{postOnActionThread})} \\
\midrule
\texttt{ah-handle-post-cmd}
  & Emits \texttt{S3PutObjReq} at the current \texttt{postObjUuid}. \\
\texttt{ah-handle-incoming-put-res-overflow}
  & Fires when \texttt{listLen(fetchableUuids) $\geq$ openObjects}.  Drops the oldest fetchable UUID, revokes its GET permission, and grants GET permission for the newly posted UUID.  Advances \texttt{postObjUuid}. \\
\texttt{ah-handle-incoming-put-res-underflow}
  & Fires when \texttt{listLen(fetchableUuids) $<$ openObjects}.  Simply grants GET permission for the posted UUID without revoking any.  Advances \texttt{postObjUuid}. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\caption{AccountHolder post-path rules implementing the fetchable UUID sliding window.}
\end{table}

\subsubsection{Acknowledgment Sinks}

Rules \texttt{ah-handle-aws-res} through \texttt{ah-handle-aws-res-4}
consume AWS response messages (\texttt{S3DeleteObjRes},
\texttt{S3PutBucketPolicyRes}, \texttt{S3CreateBucketRes},
\texttt{S3PutPublicAccessBlockRes}) that require no further state
change.

\section{Integration Guide}\label{sec:integration}

\subsection{Instantiating Actors}

To add Skyhook actors to an existing experiment configuration, define
concrete \texttt{Address} constants and construct the actors with the
required \texttt{AttributeSet}:

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={Minimal actor instantiation}]
ops puAddr ahAddr awsAddr rbAddr : -> Address .

eq myConfig =
  < puAddr : SkyhookPublicUser |
      thisAddr: puAddr,
      raceboatAddr: rbAddr,
      awsAddr: awsAddr,
      fetchBucket: "my-fetch-bucket",
      postBucket: "my-post-bucket",
      fetchObjUuid: "seed-uuid-pu-fetch",
      postObjUuid:  "seed-uuid-pu-post" >

  < ahAddr : SkyhookAccountHolder |
      thisAddr: ahAddr,
      raceboatAddr: rbAddr,
      awsAddr: awsAddr,
      fetchBucket: "my-fetch-bucket",
      postBucket:  "my-post-bucket",
      region: "us-east-1",
      puttableUuids: emptyStringList,
      fetchableUuids: emptyStringList,
      initialFetchObjUuid: "seed-uuid-ah-fetch",
      initialPostObjUuid:  "seed-uuid-ah-post",
      openObjects: 3,
      postObjUuid: "seed-uuid-ah-post" >

  < awsAddr : AwsS3 | thisAddr: awsAddr > .
\end{lstlisting}

\paragraph{Important.}  The \texttt{SkyhookStartCmd} must be sent to the
AccountHolder \emph{before} any fetch or post commands, as it
initializes the \texttt{puttableUuids} queue.

\subsection{Composing with Other Protocols}

Because Skyhook actors communicate through the standard
\texttt{(to~A from~B : Content)} message format, they can be freely
composed with other protocol actors (IRC, DNS, Mastodon, etc.) in the
same configuration soup.  The Raceboat actor dispatches
\texttt{SkyhookFetchCmd}, \texttt{SkyhookPostCmd}, and
\texttt{SkyhookStartCmd} to the appropriate Skyhook endpoints.

\section{Invariants for Model Checking}\label{sec:invariants}

The following properties can be checked with Maude's \texttt{search}
command on the nondeterministic variant:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Queue boundedness:}
    $\texttt{listLen(puttableUuids)} \leq \texttt{openObjects} + 1$
    after any reachable state.
  \item \textbf{UUID uniqueness:} No UUID appears simultaneously in
    both \texttt{puttableUuids} and \texttt{fetchableUuids}.
  \item \textbf{Liveness:} From any state where a
    \texttt{SkyhookFetchCmd} is pending and data exists at the expected
    UUID, the AccountHolder eventually delivers
    \texttt{SkyhookPostCmd(data)} to Raceboat.
\end{enumerate}
